Below are some of my personal reflections and perspectives after completing nearly three months of internship through the **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** program.

### 1. Experience with the Learning and Working Environment

The program provided a dynamic, practical, and highly collaborative learning environment for students who share the same interest in Cloud Computing. During the early stage of the internship, I encountered several challenges due to the large amount of new knowledge and the need to proactively study a significant number of technical documents in English.

However, thanks to the well-organized structure of the program, the logical learning roadmap, and the support from the community, I gradually adapted to the pace of the program. Since most activities were conducted online, I also improved my self-discipline, time management skills, and the ability to proactively communicate whenever I encountered difficulties.

### 2. Support from Mentors and the Program Management Team

The aspect I appreciated the most was the mentoring approach adopted by the mentors. Instead of providing direct solutions, they usually guided us by asking directional questions, explaining how to read logs, analyze errors, search AWS documentation, and verify each component within the system.

This approach helped me develop a systematic problem-solving mindset, become less dependent on direct guidance, and improve my ability to conduct independent research. In addition, the program management team provided comprehensive support regarding learning materials, schedules, event announcements, and administrative matters throughout the internship.

### 3. Relevance to My Major and Career Orientation

As a student majoring in **Information Technology**, specializing in **Computer Networks**, the program provided knowledge that is directly applicable to my career orientation.

Through learning Amazon VPC, EC2, IAM, API Gateway, Lambda, DynamoDB, Cognito, and WebSocket, I had the opportunity to connect my networking knowledge with the deployment of a real-world backend system in a cloud environment.

In particular, the project **"Serverless & Spot Instance Backend Architecture for Live-Service Games on AWS"** helped me gain a deeper understanding of how to build a system consisting of multiple integrated components, including user authentication, API processing, data storage, matchmaking, and real-time communication.

### 4. The Aspects I Was Most Satisfied With

* Having direct access to AWS services and practicing on a real cloud environment.
* Having the opportunity to apply knowledge to a practical project instead of stopping at theoretical concepts.
* Improving my ability to read technical documentation and independently learn new technologies.
* Gaining a better understanding of the analysis, design, implementation, and testing processes of a cloud backend system.
* Developing skills in writing worklogs, technical documentation, and structured reports.
* Connecting with other students who share the same interests in Cloud Computing, Backend Development, and DevOps.
* Participating in technical events, team activities, and knowledge-sharing sessions throughout the internship.

### 5. Suggestions for Future Programs

* **Increase the number of hands-on practice sessions:** The program could include additional Live Lab sessions with mentors, focusing on common issues such as IAM Permissions, Security Groups, API Gateway Integration, Lambda Timeouts, and VPC configuration.

* **Provide more real-world scenario exercises:** In addition to individual labs, the program could introduce project-based exercises that simulate real business requirements, encouraging students to analyze requirements and design suitable architectures independently.

* **Strengthen testing and monitoring topics:** Additional sessions covering Load Testing, CloudWatch Dashboards, Alerts, Log Analysis, and system performance evaluation would be highly beneficial.

* **Organize more career-sharing sessions:** Talks delivered by Cloud Engineers, Solutions Architects, or DevOps Engineers would help students better understand industry expectations and future career paths.

* **Create more networking opportunities among teams:** Organizing additional discussion sessions, team-building activities, or technical mini-games would encourage greater interaction and knowledge sharing among participants.

### 6. Personal Lessons Learned

Throughout the program, I realized that learning Cloud Computing is not simply about memorizing the names and functions of AWS services. More importantly, it is about understanding how different services work together and selecting the most appropriate solution for a given requirement.

I also realized that the ability to read documentation, analyze errors, and systematically troubleshoot systems is extremely important. In many situations, issues are not caused by application code but by IAM policies, Security Groups, environment variables, routing configurations, or service integrations.

Furthermore, maintaining a worklog and continuously updating technical documentation helped me manage my progress more effectively, simplify report preparation, and reflect on my own development throughout the internship.

### 7. Conclusion

The **First Cloud AI Journey** program has been a valuable experience in both my academic journey and career development. It not only provided me with AWS knowledge but also helped me strengthen my self-learning ability, problem-solving skills, and systematic approach to technology projects.

If I have the opportunity to recommend this program to other students who are interested in Cloud Computing, Backend Development, or DevOps, I would definitely encourage them to participate. It provides an excellent environment for students to gradually bridge the gap between academic knowledge and real-world industry requirements.
